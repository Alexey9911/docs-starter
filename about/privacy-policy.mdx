## Overview

ChainGuard is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard personal information when you use the ChainGuard extension and related services.

## Information we collect

### Account information

- Email address
- Name (optional)
- Payment information
- Company details for enterprise accounts

### Usage data

- API request logs
- Wallet addresses you ask us to analyze
- Feature usage patterns
- Device and browser information
- IP addresses

### Technical data

- Performance metrics
- Error logs
- Authentication tokens
- Session information

## How we use data

### Service delivery

- Provide and maintain platform functionality
- Process API requests and security analyses
- Generate analytics and insights
- Send transaction and security notifications

### Platform improvement

- Analyze usage patterns
- Improve AI models
- Optimize performance
- Develop new features

### Communication

- Send service updates and announcements
- Respond to support requests
- Deliver marketing communications if you opt in
- Share security alerts

### Compliance

- Detect and prevent fraud
- Enforce terms of service
- Comply with legal obligations
- Protect user rights and safety

## Data sharing

We do not sell personal data. Limited sharing occurs with:

### Service providers

- Cloud infrastructure providers
- Payment processors
- Analytics tools that use aggregated or anonymized data
- Email service providers

### Legal requirements

- Law enforcement requests with valid legal orders
- Compliance with court orders
- Protection of our legal rights
- Emergency situations involving safety

### Business transfers

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, user data may transfer to the new entity with continued privacy protections.

## Data security

### Technical measures

- TLS 1.3 encryption for data in transit
- AES-256 encryption for data at rest
- Multi-factor authentication support
- Regular security audits and penetration testing

### Access controls

- Role-based access limitations
- Audit logging of administrative actions
- Background checks for employees with data access

### Incident response

- 24/7 security monitoring
- Incident response procedures
- User notification within 72 hours of a confirmed breach
- Coordination with regulatory authorities

## Data retention

- **Account information**: Duration of account plus 90 days.
- **API request logs**: 90 days (Free), up to 5 years (Enterprise).
- **Billing records**: 7 years (legal requirement).
- **Support communications**: 3 years.
- **Anonymized analytics**: Indefinitely.

## Your rights

### Access and portability

You can request a copy of your personal data in a machine-readable format.

### Correction

You can update inaccurate or incomplete information through account settings or by contacting support.

### Deletion

You can request account deletion. We may retain some data to meet legal obligations.

### Opt-out

You can unsubscribe from marketing emails using the link in the footer or account preferences.

### Restriction

You can request limitation of processing for specific purposes.

## Cookies and tracking

### Essential cookies

We use essential cookies for authentication, security, and core functionality.

### Analytics cookies

We use analytics cookies to understand usage patterns and improve the service. You can disable these in your browser settings.

### Marketing cookies

We use marketing cookies only if you opt in.

## International transfers

Your data may be processed in countries outside your residence. We use appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses and data processing agreements.

## Children's privacy

ChainGuard is not intended for users under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

## California privacy rights (CCPA)

California residents have additional rights:

- Right to know what data we collect
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt out of sale of personal information (we do not sell data)
- Right to non-discrimination

Submit requests by emailing privacy@chainguardai.dev.

## European privacy rights (GDPR)

EU and EEA users have rights to:

- Access, rectification, and erasure
- Data portability
- Restrict or object to processing
- Withdraw consent
- Lodge complaints with a supervisory authority

Contact our EU representative at eu-privacy@chainguardai.dev.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy periodically. We will notify you of material changes through:

- Email to registered users
- Prominent notice on our website
- 30-day advance notice for substantial changes

Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
